Privacy Exposure

Sensitive member health data requires tighter masking, purpose-based access, consent alignment, and evidence of appropriate use.

Operational Integrity

Claims, policy, complaints, and provider workflows depend on clean reference data and consistent member matching.

Analytics Reliability

Executive reporting and actuarial-style insights are weakened where lineage, definitions, and source refresh controls are incomplete.

Third-Party Data

Provider platforms, digital partners, and service vendors introduce additional sharing, retention, and reconciliation risks.

Access Governance

Role-based access needs stronger alignment to least privilege, segregation of duties, and high-risk query monitoring.

Remediation Confidence

Most risks are addressable through ownership, lineage documentation, access reviews, and staged control uplift.

Recommended Action

Prioritise member identity, access governance, and claims-data lineage first. These risks combine high sensitivity with high operational dependency. The first 90 days should focus on narrowing privileged access, documenting end-to-end claims transformations, validating member-record matching logic, and creating evidence packs for privacy, audit, and executive risk committees. Lower-severity reference-data issues should be sequenced after the core protected-data controls are stabilised.
